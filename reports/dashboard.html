1 · The trap: Simpson’s Paradox

Insight. The promo was over-allocated to low-converting zones, so the naive aggregate (-0.09pp, CI crosses zero) makes a working campaign look dead. Re-weighting each zone by exposure recovers the true +4.46pp lift. A team reading the raw average would have killed a lever that genuinely drives conversion.

2 · Why one discount can’t fit all

Insight. New customers are highly elastic (e = -2.28) while loyal customers are inelastic (e = -0.49). Discounting loyal, price-insensitive customers is almost pure margin loss.

3 · The guardrail: does the discount pay for itself?

Insight. On thin ~25% grocery margins, every discount level reduces gross margin per exposure — the conversion win does not offset the price cut on a pure-margin basis. Pure-margin optimum: casual: 0% discount, loyal: 0% discount, new: 0% discount. Right-sizing discounts projects ~AED 2,025,852/month in recovered margin.

5 · Recommendation

1. Do not ship the flat 10% promo. It reliably lifts conversion (+4.46pp), but fails the margin guardrail in every segment (stratified margin delta AED -3.902/exposure).

2. Treat discounting as an acquisition lever, not a margin lever. If the objective is growth, target promos only at the elastic new segment — the most conversions per AED of margin spent — and justify the spend against downstream LTV, not first-order margin.

3. Stop discounting inelastic loyal customers. Reallocating discount depth by segment elasticity recovers an estimated AED 2,025,852/month.

4. Always read experiments stratified + CUPED-adjusted. The naive aggregate here was off by 50x.
